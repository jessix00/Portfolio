My Process

Strategy

All successful projects start with a good strategy. My primary focus is to define the purpose of a website while keeping in mind my clients needs and their target audience.

Research

In this phase, I begin to scout for anything that may help inspire the new website. This often includes inspiration gained from current web trends as well as my clients suggestions and preferences. It is at this point where I’ll also start to consider a technology stack that will fit with our sites needs.

Planning

Having collected enough information, I begin to put together the sitemap. A sitemap will help demonstrate how the inner structure of our website will look like. By reviewing the sitemap with my client, I get the opportunity to collect their feedback and make any necessary adjustments.

Design

This phase is all about finding the right balance between form and function. I begin by putting together the sites layout picking the appropriate fonts, colors and design motifs. While doing this, I keep in mind the end-user and their perception to the website.

Development

During this phase, all static web page elements that were designed during the layout and prototype creation will be developed and tested. Any special features and interactivity will be created as requested.

Launch

When everything has been thoroughly tested, and once my client is happy with the final product, it’s time to launch the website. Once our site goes live, I continue to run user testing on new content and features based on my clients needs.
